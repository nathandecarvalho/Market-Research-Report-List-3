<p><p>Reagent Grade Decalcifiers are typically categorized into four main types based on their composition and method of action. Inorganic Acid Based Decalcifiers work by breaking down calcium deposits using strong acids such as hydrochloric acid. Organic Acid Based Decalcifiers use milder acids like acetic acid to dissolve calcium build-up. EDTA Based Decalcifiers chelate calcium ions, forming stable complexes that can be easily washed away. Other types of decalcifiers may use alternative chemicals or methods to remove calcium deposits effectively.</p></p>
