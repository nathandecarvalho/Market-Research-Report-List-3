<p><p>Large-joint reconstructive implants include metallic, ceramic, and polymer options. Metallic implants are durable and strong, making them suitable for weight-bearing joints. Ceramic implants are known for their biocompatibility and resistance to wear. Polymers are lightweight and flexible, offering good shock absorption and reduced stress on surrounding bone. Each type of implant has its own advantages and considerations, and the choice of material depends on factors such as patient age, activity level, and specific joint requirements.</p></p>
