<p><ul><li>Extracellular Agents</li><li>Blood Pool Agents</li><li>Hepatobiliary Agents</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Gadolinium-based contrast media are used in medical imaging to enhance the visibility of tissues and organs. There are three main types: extracellular agents, which are injected into the blood and circulate outside of cells; blood pool agents, which remain in the blood for an extended period of time; and hepatobiliary agents, which are taken up by the liver and excreted into the bile. Each type of contrast agent has specific applications in MRI and other imaging procedures, based on their unique properties and behaviors within the body.</p></p>
