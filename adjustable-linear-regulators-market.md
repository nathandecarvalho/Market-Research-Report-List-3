<p><p>Single-channel adjustable linear regulators are designed to regulate the output voltage of a single electronic component, providing precise and stable voltage levels. On the other hand, Multi-channel adjustable linear regulators have multiple independent channels that can regulate the output voltage for multiple electronic components simultaneously. This allows for more flexibility and control in managing different voltage requirements within a single system. Both types of regulators are commonly used in various electronic devices to ensure consistent and reliable power supply.</p></p>
