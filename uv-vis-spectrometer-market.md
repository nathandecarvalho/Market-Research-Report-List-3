<p><p>A UV-Vis spectrometer is a device used to measure the absorption or transmission of light by a sample at different wavelengths in the ultraviolet-visible range. In the single-beam market, the sample and reference are measured one after the other, while in the double-beam market, both are measured simultaneously. Single-beam spectrometers are simpler and more cost-effective, while double-beam spectrometers are more accurate and offer better stability. Both types have their own advantages and are chosen based on the specific requirements of the user.</p></p>
