<p><p>Two-phase immersion cooling is a method of cooling electronics by submerging them in a two-phase liquid, typically water or oil. In the water-based market, the cooling liquid used is typically water, which has a high heat capacity and is non-flammable. In the oil-based market, the cooling liquid used is typically a dielectric oil, which has a higher boiling point than water and is often preferred for high-temperature applications. Both types of immersion cooling offer efficient and effective cooling solutions for electronic devices.</p></p>
