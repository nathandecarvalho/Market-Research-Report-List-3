<p><p>Non-contact extensometers are devices used in material testing to measure strain or deformation in a specimen without physically touching it. There are two common types of non-contact extensometers: laser extensometers and video extensometers. Laser extensometers use a laser beam to measure displacement, while video extensometers use high-resolution cameras to capture images of a specimen and analyze changes in its shape. Both types offer high accuracy and precision in measuring strain, making them valuable tools in research and quality control applications.</p></p>
