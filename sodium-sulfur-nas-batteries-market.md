<p><p>Sodium Sulfur (NaS) batteries are primarily categorized into two types in the market: Molten-Salt Type and Others. Molten-salt type batteries involve a solid sulfur cathode, liquid sodium anode, and a solid electrolyte separator, offering high energy density and long cycle life. On the other hand, the "Others" category includes variations that may use different materials for the electrodes and electrolytes, catering to specific applications or performance requirements. Both types aim to provide efficient and reliable energy storage solutions.</p></p>
