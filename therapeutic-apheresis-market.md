<p><p>Therapeutic Apheresis is a medical procedure that involves removing specific components from a patient's blood and returning the rest to their body. Two common types of Therapeutic Apheresis are Therapeutic Plasma Exchange and Red Blood Cell Exchange. Therapeutic Plasma Exchange involves removing and replacing the plasma in a patient's blood, often used to treat conditions like autoimmune disorders. Red Blood Cell Exchange involves replacing a patient's red blood cells with donor red blood cells, typically used to treat conditions like sickle cell disease or severe malaria.</p></p>
