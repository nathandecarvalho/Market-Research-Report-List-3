<p><p>A Scanning Tunneling Microscope (STM) is a specialized microscopy technique that is used to obtain atomic-scale images of surfaces. STM works by scanning a sharp metal tip very close to the surface of a sample, allowing electrons to tunnel between the tip and the sample. This tunneling current is highly sensitive to the distance between the tip and the sample, allowing for the creation of detailed images at the atomic level.</p><p>The STM market is experiencing significant growth, with a projected CAGR of 11.8% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to a rising demand for STM in various industries, including nanotechnology, materials science, and semiconductor research. <p><p>Scanning Tunneling Microscopes (STM) are used for high-resolution imaging of surfaces at the atomic level. Two common market types for STMs are Constant-height Mode and Constant-current Mode. In Constant-height Mode, the tip is kept at a fixed distance from the sample surface, providing topographic information. In Constant-current Mode, the tunneling current between the tip and the sample is kept constant, allowing for variations in tip-sample distance to be detected. Each mode has its own advantages and applications in the study of materials at the nano scale.</p></p>
