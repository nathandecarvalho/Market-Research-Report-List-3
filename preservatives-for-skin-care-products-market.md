<p><p>Preservatives for skin care products are categorized into different types based on their chemical composition and effectiveness in inhibiting microbial growth. Lipids, such as oils and waxes, create a barrier that prevents bacteria and fungi from proliferating in cosmetics. Acids, like benzoic acid and sorbic acid, lower the pH of the product, making it inhospitable for microbes. Alcohols, such as ethanol and isopropanol, disrupt the cell membranes of microorganisms. Other preservatives, like parabens and formaldehyde releasers, have different mechanisms of action to extend the shelf life of skin care products.</p></p>
