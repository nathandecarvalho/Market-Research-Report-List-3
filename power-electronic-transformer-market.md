<p><p>Power electronic transformers are devices used to convert voltage levels in power systems. In single-stage power electronic transformers, voltage conversion is achieved in a single stage, making them simpler and more cost-effective. Two-stage power electronic transformers involve voltage conversion in two stages, providing higher efficiency and flexibility in controlling power flow. Three-stage power electronic transformers further increase efficiency and allow for more advanced power system control. Each type of power electronic transformer offers different advantages in terms of cost, efficiency, and control capabilities.</p></p>
